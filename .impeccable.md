## Design Context

### Users
This site is publicly visible, but it is primarily for the owner. It serves as a personal running archive: a place to review training history, study long-term patterns, and preserve a coherent record of runs over time. Visitors may browse it, but the core use case is self-reflection and analysis rather than broad social sharing.

### Brand Personality
The interface should feel simple, hard-edged, and light.

Three-word personality:
- Minimal
- Rigorous
- Airy

Emotional goals:
- Support focused review rather than passive scrolling
- Feel trustworthy and precise without looking like a dashboard product
- Keep the experience breathable so dense training data does not become oppressive

### Aesthetic Direction
The visual direction is technical, but not stereotypically geeky. It should not resemble an admin panel, a hacker-themed interface, or a neon-dark developer toy. The design should balance analytical clarity with calm restraint: structured information, strong hierarchy, clean geometry, and generous breathing room.

Theme:
- Both light and dark themes should be supported

Anti-references:
- Do not make it look like a backend console
- Do not lean into overtly nerdy UI tropes
- Avoid fluorescent accents, cyberpunk hues, and glow-heavy dark themes

### Design Principles
1. Treat the page as a personal performance archive, not a product dashboard.
2. Build technical clarity through structure, typography, and spacing rather than sci-fi decoration.
3. Keep dense information breathable with light composition and deliberate negative space.
4. Make analysis feel sharp and exact, but never cold or visually noisy.
5. Ensure both light and dark themes preserve the same restrained, athletic character.
